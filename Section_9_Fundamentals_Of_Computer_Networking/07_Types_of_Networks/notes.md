## Types of Networks

1. `LAN (Local Area Network)`
   - **Scope**: Small area like a home, school, or office.

   - **Range**: A few meters to a few kilometers.

   - **Examples**: Home Wi-Fi, office computer network.

   - **Use**: Fast, secure, low-cost internal communication.

2. ` MAN (Metropolitan Area Network)`
   - **Scope**: City-level, connects multiple LANs within a city.

   - **Range**: Up to ~50 km.

   - **Examples**: City-wide broadband, cable TV networks.

   - **Use**: Communication between schools, offices, or institutions in one city.

3. `WAN (Wide Area Network)`
   - **Scope**: Covers large geographical areas, even countries or continents.

   - **Range**: Thousands of kilometers.

   - **Examples**: The Internet, nationwide bank ATM networks.

   - **Use**: Global communication, connecting large businesses.

4. `PAN (Personal Area Network)`
   - **Scope**: Very short range, around one person.

   - **Range**: ~10 meters.

   - **Examples**: Bluetooth, mobile tethering, smartwatch syncing.

5. `BAN (Body Area Network)`
   - **Scope**: Around the human body, mainly for healthcare devices.

   - **Range**: A few centimeters to meters.

   - **Examples**: Heart rate monitors, fitness trackers.

6. `CAN (Campus Area Network)`
   - **Scope**: Multiple LANs across a campus (college, university, or company).

   - **Range**: Larger than LAN, smaller than MAN (few kilometers).

   - **Examples**: University campus network, corporate campus network.
